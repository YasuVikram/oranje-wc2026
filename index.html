How to read this

What you're looking at. Pick any of the top-10 contenders above; the row of tiles is that team's most likely route through the knockout stage — Round of 32 on the left, the Final on the right. Each tile is one match: the date and CET kick-off are fixed, the opponent is the most probable team for that slot, and the % is their chance of reaching that round (the trophy tile shows the chance of winning the whole thing).

The assumption. Each path assumes the team wins its group (the top-seed route). Finishing 2nd would drop them onto the other side of the draw — usually a tougher path (see below).

Opponents. The bracket slots are fixed by FIFA, so early opponents are fairly certain; deeper rounds assume the favourite advances, so the names get less certain — shown by the confidence tag. Round-of-32 slots marked "3rd-place team" are filled by a best-third qualifier once all groups finish.

The percentages are anchored to the Opta supercomputer's published figures — each team's chance to advance from the group and to win the tournament — with equal-strength steps assumed for the rounds in between. They show the shape of the climb, not a precise forecast.
